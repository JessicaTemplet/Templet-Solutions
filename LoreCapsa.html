What Lore does

Every tool a writer wishes existed, in one place.

Self-Populating Story Bible

Import your manuscript and Lore reads it. Characters, locations, world rules, and lore facts populate automatically — confirmed by you, never imposed.

Character Intelligence

Track appearance, personality, wounds, arcs, and relationships. Know what every character knows at every point in the story — and what they don't.

Plot & Subplot Tracking

Open threads, quiet threads, and paid-off threads. Lore flags when a subplot you opened in chapter 2 hasn't moved by chapter 8.

Corkboard View

See your entire story spatially. Drag scenes, rearrange structure, spot pacing gaps before you feel them on the page.

Continuity Checking

Lore knows when a character acts on knowledge they shouldn't have yet — and when a veil you set in act one is accidentally pierced in act two.

Killed Darlings Vault

Cut scenes, abandoned arcs, renamed characters — nothing is truly deleted. The vault keeps everything searchable, restorable, and connected to what it once touched.

Knowledge Asymmetry Tracking

Track what the reader knows versus what each character knows. The gap between them is where tension lives — Lore maps it precisely.

Character Wound & Arc Mechanics

Trauma, flaws, and healing aren't a straight line. Track the state of each wound at each story beat — too perfect loses the audience, too broken loses their affection.

Series Management

One shelf for every book. Characters, lore, and continuity accumulate across the entire series so Book 3 inherits everything Book 1 established.

How it works

From manuscript to intelligence in minutes.

Import your manuscript

Upload any .docx, .txt, .epub, or .pdf. Lore detects chapters, strips editor notes, and structures your manuscript automatically.

The bible populates

Characters, locations, and world rules are extracted in the background. You confirm what's right, correct what's wrong. Each confirmation improves future imports.

The graph builds

Every entity connects to every other entity. Lore builds a belief graph of your world — coherent, queryable, and contradiction-aware.

Write with clarity

Query your own story. Find every scene Soren appears in. Check what Faye knew before the revelation. Trace the arc of a wound across the whole series.
